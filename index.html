About Spartan Flight

Spartan Flight is San José State University's student-run unmanned aerial systems team. We compete annually in the Student Unmanned Aerial Systems (SUAS) Competition against 70+ international teams. Building a complete autonomous system is as much an engineering challenge as it is a financial one.

Our Teams

40+ members participate in one of four distinct subteams, each bringing specialized expertise to our mission.

Mechanical

Designs and manufactures lightweight, reliable composite aircraft. Responsible for structural analysis, CAD modeling, payload mechanisms, and landing systems to meet competition requirements.

Electrical

Manages all electronics systems: power distribution, PCB design, sensor integration, telemetry systems, and custom battery management. Creates the robust electronics backbone for flight success.

Software

Designs autonomous flight systems including path planning, computer vision, obstacle avoidance, and ground station control. Enables fully autonomous mission execution.

Business

Manages sponsorships, funding, and team outreach. Handles marketing, graphics, social media, and partnerships to support our mission and grow the team.

Our Projects

We tackle the full spectrum of autonomous aerial systems through practical engineering challenges.

Current Initiatives

  • Custom quadcopter airframe design
  • Target detection via computer vision
  • Autonomous payload delivery
  • Custom Li-ion battery packs & BMS
  • Low voltage power distribution
  • Photogrammetry with OpenCV

R&D Initiatives

  • VTOL design for extended range
  • FPGA computer vision acceleration
  • GPS-denied localization systems
  • Swarm multi-agent coordination
  • Coordination with ground obstacle avoidance systems
